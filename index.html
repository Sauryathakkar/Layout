Temghar Dam
Temghar Dam, is an earthfill and gravity dam on Mutha river near Mulashi, Pune district in the state of Maharashtra in India..
Location: Mulashi, Lavasa, Maharashtra
Bamboosa
Bamboosa in Lavasa is a cultural center and handicraft factory that showcases the use of bamboo, a material abundant in the surrounding forests. Visitors can observe local artisans crafting various bamboo products, purchase items like furniture and decor, and even buy saplings from the on-site nursery. The initiative aims to promote bamboo products and provide employment for the local rural population.
Location: Survey No. 60, Mugaon Lavasa, Post Pathershet, Taluka Mulshi, Pune, India.
Devkund Waterfall
Devkund Waterfall layered with frondescence, and water gushing out from the forest, it is not to be left out while visiting other Lavasa sightseeing destinations. The height of the falls ranges around 220 ft. with unknown depth; hence, travellers are advised to play in the shallow water.

Dasve viewpoint
The Dasve Viewpoint offers picturesque vistas of the surrounding biodiversity of Lavasa. It is quite famous among photographers due to the unparalleled panoramic views that the site offers. From here, you can admire the lush greenery on the mountain ranges, the splendid lakes, and the beautiful rivers.
Location: SH 57, Lavasa, Maharashtra
Mulshi Dam
Mulshi Dam, the term Mulshi is derived from a small village at the center of the Mulshi Valley. Placed at the periphery of the town, it is recognized as an eco-tourism hotspot. The dam is famous for its waterfalls, bird-watching, trekking, and for unlimited sightseeing.
Location: Mulshi Taluk, Pune which is almost 60 km from Lavasa.
Tamhini Ghat
Visiting the most alluring Tamhini Ghat is yet another dazzling experience to all tourists. Surrounded by cascading waters, lakes and dense woods. Located on the crest of Sahyadris, this green passage is well known for a long drive, featured with high mountains, and green valley on both sides.
Location: This mountain pass is located between Tamhini and Mulshi lake, which is 69 km away from Lavasa.

Quick Facts about Lavasa

  • Lavasa is India’s first planned hill city, inspired by the Italian town Portofino.
  • It’s located about 65 km from Pune and around 200 km from Mumbai.
  • The city enjoys a pleasant climate all year round — perfect for weekend getaways.
  • Popular for cycling, boating, and trekking
  • Best Time to Visit: September to March — when the weather is cool, misty, and perfect for sightseeing.
