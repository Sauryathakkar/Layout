History of Lavasa

Lavasa is a planned hill city inspired by the Italian town of Portofino. Conceived in the early 2000s, it was designed as a modern, eco-friendly destination blending natural beauty with urban living. Nestled along the banks of the Varasgaon Lake and surrounded by the Western Ghats, it offers scenic landscapes, lakeside promenades, and vibrant townships. Today, Lavasa is a popular getaway for leisure, adventure, and cultural experiences.

A Blend of Nature and Modern Living

Built around the serene Varasgaon Lake, Lavasa offers a perfect mix of modern infrastructure and natural beauty. The colorful buildings, clean roads, and refreshing mountain air make it a favorite weekend destination for travelers.
